== Terms

This section contains brief descriptions of some features commonly available
in cars these days. It is for minimum understanding and is likely to be
inaccurate and incomplete.

- *Automatic Headlamps* This is about automatic detection of low light and
  turning on of headlamps (and possibly other lights).
- *Cruise Control*
- *Automatic boot lid* Also called *electric tailgate opening*, this is about
  pressing a button to open or close the rear boot.
- *Wireless connectivity* This is used in reference to Android Auto and Apple
  CarPlay. It is about the smartphone connecting to the car without the use of
  cables - through bluetooth and Wi-Fi.
- *Tweeters* These are speakers designed to reproduce high-frequency sound.
- Passive entry passive start (PEPS) Passive entry is the functionality to
  open the car when the key is near and the door handle is touched. It is a
  convenience feature that might be particularly useful for women drivers who
  typically don't have pockets in their dresses. However, attackers can
  amplify the key signal from a distance. It will be useful to know if
  key-less entry can be disabled. Passive start is about starting the engine
  using a push button.

== Tata Tigor

Why?

- Good visibility
- It is narrower than other similar cars like Maruti Dzire, Maruti Swift and
  Honda Amaze. This is expected to make parking easier. Most of the driving
  will be in the city.
- Decent headroom
- It makes many safety features like anti break locking system (ABS) and
  electronic breakforce distribution (EBD), corner stability control (CSC),
  electronic stability program (ESP), rear parking sensors and reverse parking
  camera available to the budget buyer.
- Comfortable rear sitting experience
- Moderate (not flashy) interiors
- People appear to report good mileage of 15-16 kmpl even in the city
- Higher ground clearance of 170 mm
- Large boot space of over 400 litres

Why not?

- It is possible that Tata's service centres don't offer the same kind of
  smooth experience as that of Maruti. The number of service centre visits is
  just around 1-2 per year. One can decide to remain stoic during those
  visits.
- One will have to adapt to the lower sitting position of a sedan.

== Cost of Ownership

The cost of owning a car for 15 years can be approximated as follows:

- *Car* This is fixed and has to be paid in the beginning: 8,50,000
- *Maintenance* Maintenance tends to be lower in the initial 5 years and then
  goes up. Assuming it to be 10,000 per year: 1,50,000
- *Insurance* Insurance is highest at the time of buying and then keeps
  decreasing as the car becomes old. Assuming an average of 15,000 per year:
  2,25,000
- *Petrol* Petrol expenditure involves usage (kilometers) and cost. Assuming
  total cost to be 7,000/month: 12,60,00
- *Total* The total cost of ownership gets computed as Rs 24,85,000 or Rs
  14,000/month or Rs 455/day.
